# qaai_system — Architecture Constitution (Phases A–M)

## Purpose
This document defines the **immutable architectural laws** governing qaai_system.
These laws supersede implementation details, models, and strategies.

Violation of any law invalidates system correctness.

---

## Core Doctrine
**Stability → Adaptivity → Intelligence**

---

## Phase A — Determinism
All system behavior must be reproducible given the same inputs.
No hidden randomness, no implicit state.

---

## Phase B — Broker Agnosticism
Strategies, risk, and intelligence layers must not contain broker-specific logic.

---

## Phase C — Capital Safety First
Capital protection overrides performance, intelligence, and opportunity.

---

## Phase D — Declarative State Transitions
All lifecycle changes must be explicit, logged, and guarded.

---

## Phase E — Evidence-Based Promotion
Strategies advance only via documented, empirical proof.

---

## Phase F — Failure Visibility
Failures are first-class signals and must be observable.

---

## Phase G — Governance Over Automation
Human approval is mandatory for all risk-bearing transitions.

---

## Phase H — Historical Replayability
The system must be able to explain any past decision.

---

## Phase I — Intelligence Is Advisory
ML may inform but never command.

---

## Phase J — Justified Decisions
Every decision must have a recorded reason.

---

## Phase K — Knowledge Isolation
Layers may only know what they strictly require.

---

## Phase L — Lifecycle Supremacy
Lifecycle state overrides all other signals.

---

## Phase M — Minimal Mutation
State mutation must be rare, explicit, and auditable.

---

## Closing Statement
These principles are permanent.
All future phases must comply.
